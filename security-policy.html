Security Policy

Last updated: 2025-11-12

We take the security of our users, research outputs, and infrastructure seriously. Responsible disclosure allows us to coordinate fixes and protect the community.

Scope

This policy covers publicly reachable assets under the toppymicros.com domain and our public GitHub repositories under ToppyMicroServices. Internal staging systems, employee accounts, and third-party platforms are out of scope.

Out of Scope / Non-Issues

  • Best-practice recommendations without a clear exploit path
  • Missing security headers that do not enable a direct vulnerability
  • Clickjacking on non-authenticated pages
  • Use of known-vulnerable libraries without a proven exploit chain
  • Self XSS or issues requiring modifying browser/dev tools
  • Denial of service via volumetric traffic (report to host/network provider)

How to Report

Email security@toppymicros.com with a concise report including:

  1. Vulnerability description and affected asset
  2. Reproduction steps (minimal PoC)
  3. Impact assessment (what could an attacker achieve)
  4. Any suggested remediation (optional)

Researcher Commitments

  • Do not exfiltrate more data than necessary to prove the issue.
  • Do not access, modify, or delete user data.
  • Do not disrupt services or degrade availability.
  • Give us a reasonable window (30â€“60 days) to remediate before disclosure.
  • Use good faith and comply with applicable law.

Our Commitments

  • Acknowledge receipt within 5 business days.
  • Provide a triage result and planned next steps after initial validation.
  • Keep you updated at meaningful remediation milestones.
  • Offer public thanks (Hall of Thanks) if desired after resolution.

Severity / Prioritization

We loosely map to CVSS-style impact dimensions (confidentiality, integrity, availability). Critical issues affecting user data or enabling remote code execution receive highest priority. Informational issues are tracked but may not be prioritized.

Coordinated Disclosure Timeline

We aim to remediate most valid issues within 30 days. Complex architecture issues may require up to 60 days. We collaborate on extensions if meaningful progress is underway.

Safe Harbor

If you make a good faith effort to comply with this policy while researching, we will not pursue legal action. Activities should cease once you confirm the presence of a vulnerability. Avoid persistence or lateral movement.

Hall of Thanks

We plan to publish (opt-in) acknowledgements for researchers who help improve our security posture. Let us know if you prefer anonymity.

Thesis

AI failures are boundary failures

Most AI failures are not only model failures. They are boundary failures: unclear ownership, invisible escalation paths, drifting evaluation logic, and unsafe tool surfaces.

Ownership gets vague

Teams ship model-mediated decisions before naming who owns approvals, exceptions, and post-launch review.

Evaluation logic drifts

Benchmarks, prompts, thresholds, and business rules move quietly unless the evaluation boundary is explicit.

Escalation paths hide

Human review becomes performative when the workflow does not state when a model must stop, defer, or log risk.

Tool surfaces expand

Useful automation becomes risky when permissions, files, network calls, and data retention are broader than the task needs.

Wedge

A narrow evidence layer for small teams

Our wedge is evaluation evidence for small technical teams: a narrow surface where model behavior, human review, and security boundaries meet.

Why now

Model adoption moved faster than responsibility design. Large-enterprise MLOps is often too heavy, while small teams still need tests, logs, approvals, and boundary rules they can operate.

Best-fit teams

  • B2B SaaS teams shipping AI features before evaluation criteria are stable.
  • Operators using LLMs internally without clear approval and audit boundaries.
  • Small technical organizations that need automation inside security-sensitive environments.

What we are not

  • Not an AI automation agency.
  • Not prompt magic or dashboard theatre.
  • Not risk hidden behind optimistic demos.

Services

What you can hire Toppy for

Open contact page

Services are delivery formats for the same thesis: make model behavior, human responsibility, and software boundaries visible enough to review.

Evaluation evidence sprint

Define what the model may decide, what it must never decide, which tests matter, and what unresolved failures remain.

Typical outputs: Test cases, severity labels, pass/fail evidence, calibration notes, and unresolved-risk list.

AI workflow boundary review

Map who approves, what the model can suggest, when humans must intervene, and which events need durable logs.

Typical outputs: Approval map, escalation rules, ownership boundaries, and implementation priorities.

Security-bounded tool surface

Design or ship narrow internal tools where permissions, network access, file handling, and retention match the job.

Typical outputs: Feature policy, enablement gates, data-handling boundaries, and release checklist.

Sample artifact

Example: failure-mode matrix

A project output should be specific enough to review. A lightweight matrix can show the decision boundary, test evidence, escalation rule, and unresolved risk in one place.

Model action Drafts a customer-facing answer from internal policy notes.
Failure mode Overstates policy certainty when source text is partial or conflicting.
Test case Ambiguous customer request with two plausible policy interpretations.
Escalation rule Human approval required when confidence is low, policy references conflict, or customer impact is material.
Logged evidence Prompt version, retrieved sources, model answer, reviewer decision, and unresolved risk.
